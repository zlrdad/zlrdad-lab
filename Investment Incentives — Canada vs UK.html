Two Models of Investor Behaviour

FTS Model

Month 0 Subscribe to FTS; claim deduction
Month 6–18 Hold or exit (tax arbitrage window)
Month 18–24 Exit before speculation risk peaks
Pattern: "Subscribe → Deduct → Exit" tax arbitrage cycle. Average holding: 18–24 months.

Outcome (Jog 2016): Investors lost ~$1.2B on $2.5B deployed (48% value destruction)

EIS/SEIS Model

Year 0 Invest; claim 30%–50% credit
Years 1–3 Company scales; investor holds
Year 3+ Capital gains exemption unlocked; can exit tax-free
Pattern: "Invest → Hold 3+ years → Grow or claim loss relief." Alignment with company lifecycle.

Outcome: 46.5% of UK unicorns funded by EIS; 18.3% IRR on platform portfolios

What Canada Is Missing

UK Features

  • Income tax credit (30%–50%)
  • Capital gains exemption
  • Capital gains deferral (rollover relief)
  • Loss relief against income
  • Crowdfunding platform integration
  • Sector breadth (all qualifying trades)

Canada Gaps

  • No income tax credit for innovation
  • No capital gains exemption (nil cost base)
  • No capital gains deferral
  • No loss relief against income
  • No crowdfunding integration
  • Mining & energy only

The Path Forward

Three policy steps to modernize Canada's venture investment incentives:

1

Innovation FTS

Extend flow-through share deduction to SR&ED-eligible companies in technology, cleantech, life sciences, and advanced manufacturing — not just minerals.

2

Add EIS Features

Layer income tax credits (20%–30%), capital gains exemption on 3+ year holds, and loss relief against other income — creating alignment incentives.

3

Platform Integration

Connect with OSC-regulated equity crowdfunding platforms to lower friction and bring retail investors into innovation ventures at scale.
