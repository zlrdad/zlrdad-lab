Tax environment trade-offs: Canada's R&D tax credit (SR&ED) is competitive in nominal terms but complicated and slow to administer. Capital gains tax (~13-27% effective at 50% inclusion) lacks any innovation-specific relief equivalent to QSBS (US), EIS/SEIS (UK), or ESIC (Australia). Policy priority for Canada: streamline SR&ED, introduce founder-specific CGT relief or QSBS equivalent, and expand investor-side incentives (AITD).

Founder HQ Retention — Canada
% of Canadian-led high-potential startups HQ'd in Canada (2024). Comparator data is qualitative only — pending research.
Founder flight crisis: Only 32.4% of Canadian-led high-potential startups launched in 2024 were HQ'd in Canada — down from >67% in 2015-19 (Leaders Fund 2025). Israel, Sweden, and Singapore retain founders through ecosystem density, tax incentives, and quality-of-life mechanisms. The US is the primary destination. Policy response: (1) introduce founder CGT relief or QSBS equivalent, (2) VCCI + LTAF expansion, (3) modernize startup visa (currently 52 months, program paused Jan 2026).
Data: Dealroom, Bain 2024, Leaders Fund, RBC Economics, StatCan. Full citations in the International Comparator Data Compendium.

The angel and crowdfunding gap: South Korea offers 100% tax deduction (up to ₩30M), the UK's SEIS provides 50% upfront relief, and Singapore's AITD gives 50%. Canada has no angel-specific tax incentive — the only G7 country in this position. Canada's value conversion failure: Canada invests at a level consistent with a 13th-ranked innovation economy but converts at the level of a 20th-ranked one — a 7-rank efficiency gap. Policy bottleneck analysis: Canada has many policies but implementation lags comparators in speed, simplicity, and effectiveness. Key gaps: visa processing (52 months vs. 10 days in Israel; program paused Jan 2026), no LTAF framework (UK has £252B in scope), lowest crowdfunding investor cap (C$2,500 vs. US $124K), no angel/investor tax incentive (UK EIS/SEIS: £1.8B/yr), and no founder CGT relief equivalent to QSBS (US) or ESIC (Australia). Recommendation: (1) introduce EIS/SEIS-style investor tax incentive, (2) create LTAF framework, (3) modernize immigration (8-week target), (4) raise crowdfunding caps, (5) introduce founder CGT relief.
