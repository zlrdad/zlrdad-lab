What's Missing

Canada has built platforms but not the ecosystem incentives that drive participation:

✗
Tax incentive for equity crowdfunding investors
✗
Nominee structure for simplified share management
✗
Secondary market for trading crowdfunded shares
✗
Integration with investment tax schemes
✗
Platform-managed SEIS/EIS-style compliance
The Virtuous Cycle: The UK built a system where tax incentives → more investors → more companies → better returns → more investors. Canada has the platforms but not the incentives.

The Opportunity

What could change with coordinated policy:

$500M+
Estimated annual new startup investment if Canada adopted an EIS-equivalent

Combined with NI 45-111 (self-certified investor exemption), tax incentives could democratize early-stage investing in Canada

Policy Path: Introduce a modest tax credit (20-30%) for equity crowdfunding investments up to $50K annually, modeled on the UK's EIS structure but adapted for Canada's regulatory framework. This would attract retail capital from the 2-3M Canadians already comfortable backing projects on Kickstarter.
